Logic Normal Forms

William Schultz

William Schultz

  • Conjunctive normal form (CNF): a conjunction of disjunctions. More precisely, a conjunction of clauses, where a clause is a disjunction of literals e.g. $$\begin{aligned} (a \vee b) \wedge (b \vee \neg c) \wedge (a \vee d) \end{aligned}$$ Also called product of sums form.

  • Disjunctive normal form (DNF): a disjunction of conjunctions. Also described as a disjunction of cubes, where a cube is a conjunction of literals. e.g. $$\begin{aligned} (a \wedge b) \vee (b \wedge \neg c) \vee (a \wedge d) \end{aligned}$$ Also called sum of products form.
